Product Ordering Methods

How Compify decides the order in which products are presented to each judge.

━ Linear

Products are presented in the same fixed numerical order to every judge — bottle 1, bottle 2, bottle 3. The classic setup: simple, predictable, and easy for stewards to manage on the floor. Every judge evaluates the same sequence, making logistics straightforward and coordination minimal.

Ideal for: Traditional in-person competitions, sommelier exams, small panels with a fixed tasting flight

⟠ Randomized

Each judge receives products in a completely unique random order, generated by Compify at the start of the session. This eliminates position bias — no product is systematically penalized by always being tasted first or last. Particularly effective when the back office controls the serving sequence or when judges evaluate remotely on their own devices.

Ideal for: Online competitions, remote judging, events where the back office defines the product sequence

◆ Algorithm-guided

Compify's proprietary algorithm builds a custom tasting order for each judge, optimizing across multiple dimensions simultaneously: it separates products from the same producer, alternates between categories to allow palate recovery, avoids clustering similar styles, and ensures statistical fairness across the full panel. The result is a tasting path that maximizes evaluation accuracy — something no manual planning could achieve at scale.

Ideal for: High-stakes international competitions, large panels (50+ entries), multi-category events requiring maximum precision
